What should a photographer put on their business card?

Wondering what to include on your photography business card? This blog post covers all the essentials, from your name and contact information to your website and social media handles.

26th Oct, 2023
A business card for photographers is a way to network, share their contact information, or a way to ask for a referral. So, what exactly should a photographer put on their business card so that people can remember them?
 
Let's dive into the world of visiting cards for photographers and uncover the elements that will leave a lasting imprint on potential clients and fellow creatives alike.
 

Business cards for photographers: what to include?

 
1. Your name and title:

It is obvious that your business card will have your name and your title as a professional. Make sure to add your specialty, for example, if you specialize in a specific area of photography, such as weddings, landscapes, wildlife, etc., include that information on your business card like “Senior Wedding Photographer”, “Wildlife Photographer”, etc.
 

 2. Contact details:

Contact information is also one of the basic but important things on a photographer's business card. Your contact information should consist of the following:
 
  • Phone number: A direct approach for potential customers to contact you is by phone, so you should give your active phone number. If you have a Skype account, include that for the ease of customers abroad.
 
  • Email address: Include a business-related email address to represent your photography business.

 3. Address:

Consider the scope of your photography business. If you primarily work locally and want to attract clients from your immediate area, including the office or studio address can be beneficial, so that you can have a meeting or do a photoshoot in person. On the other hand, if you serve a broader, national, or international clientele, the address may be less relevant.

 
4. Logo and branding:

Consistency in branding is key. You should incorporate your logo and brand colors onto your business card to reinforce your professional identity. Your logo should be visually appealing and reflect the essence of your photography style. You can also have your visiting card’s look and feel in line with your specialty. Like, if you are a wedding photographer, your visiting card can be designed accordingly.

 
5. Services offered:

A brief list of the photography services you provide can be a helpful addition to your business card. You can mention on the card that you offer pre-wedding shoots, weddings, portraits, etc., or that you offer additional services such as videography, printing photos, and making physical albums.

 
6. Social media URLs:

You should print the URLs of your social media accounts on your business card so that potential customers can check them out. This can help increase visibility, gain new followers, and ultimately lead to more bookings.


7. QR code of your digital business card: 

 
As a photographer, the easiest way to showcase your skill is by showcasing your portfolio. You can easily achieve this via the wCard digital business card, where you can show your portfolio, both in the form of photos as well as videos. To connect your paper visiting card to your digital business card, you can print the QR code of your digital business card over your paper visiting card. So, your potential client can easily scan it and immediately check your portfolio.
